Fuel Tender Specification

RFP framework for jet-fuel supply negotiations

DN CONSULTANCY

Use this framework to structure a competitive fuel-supply tender. It sets out the terms that matter most to unit-cost outcomes, based on DN's negotiation experience across East African carriers. It does not replace legal review of the resulting contract.

1. Scope of supply

  • Airports / stations covered, with into-plane provider named per station
  • Estimated annual uplift (litres) per station and total
  • Contract term (recommend 12–24 months with a review point)
  • Minimum/maximum volume commitments, if any

2. Commercial terms to specify

  • Index basis — Platts (specify grade/basis) or local posted price
  • Differential structure — fixed vs. sliding-scale by volume
  • Into-plane fee — quoted separately from the differential
  • Payment terms — credit days, currency, FX risk allocation
  • Taxes & levies — confirm what is/isn't included

3. Evaluation criteria (suggested weighting)

  • All-in price at forecast volume — 40%
  • Supply reliability / into-plane coverage — 25%
  • Credit terms & working-capital impact — 15%
  • Hedging flexibility & price-risk tools offered — 10%
  • Contingency / force-majeure provisions — 10%

4. Risk & hedging provisions

  • Right to hedge independently vs. supplier-managed hedging
  • Price-cap or collar options and their cost
  • Force-majeure and supply-disruption remedies
  • Benchmark audit rights (right to verify index pricing)

5. Timeline template

  • Week 1–2: RFP issued to shortlisted suppliers
  • Week 3–4: Bids received, technical evaluation
  • Week 5: Commercial negotiation with top 2 bidders
  • Week 6: Award and contract execution
