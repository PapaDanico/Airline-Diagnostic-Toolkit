Methodology & Sources

How the Airline Health Scorecard measures, weights and interprets your answers

In one sentence. The Scorecard asks 40 evidence-anchored questions across 8 operational and commercial domains, scores each answer 0–4 against maturity descriptors, weights the domains by their typical impact on airline viability, and converts the result into a 0–100 Health Index with domain-level gap analysis.

1. Why a structured diagnostic?

Airline underperformance is rarely caused by a single failure. It emerges from the interaction of safety culture, schedule reliability, fleet economics, cost discipline and commercial execution. A structured, weighted diagnostic surfaces which combination of gaps is holding an operator back — before a consulting engagement, not after weeks of discovery. The Scorecard is the same instrument DN uses to open client engagements, published here in self-service form.

2. The 8 domains and their weights

Weights reflect each domain's typical influence on an airline's regulatory standing, cash generation and resilience. They were calibrated from DN's engagement history with African and Middle-East carriers and cross-checked against the priorities embedded in ICAO, IATA and lessor due-diligence frameworks.

3. How scoring works

  • Each domain has 5 questions; each answer maps to a maturity level from 0 (absent/ad-hoc) to 4 (optimised/industry-leading).
  • A domain score is the average of its 5 answers, expressed as a percentage of the maximum.
  • The Health Index is the weighted sum of the 8 domain percentages using the weights above.
  • Answer options are written as observable descriptors ("we have X, reviewed at Y cadence") rather than self-ratings, to reduce optimism bias.

4. Interpretation bands

0–44 · Turnaround required 45–64 · Material gaps to close 65–79 · Competitive, with upside 80–100 · Best-in-class trajectory

The report also flags your three widest domain gaps and prescribes the matching DN toolboxes and engagement phases, so the output is a prioritised action shortlist rather than a raw number.

5. Frameworks and sources we draw on

  • ICAO Annex 19 & Doc 9859 (SMM) — safety management maturity descriptors.
  • IATA IOSA standards and IATA economics briefings — operational and cost benchmarks.
  • ACI / IATA on-time performance conventions — reliability definitions (OTP-15, completion factor).
  • Standard airline unit-economics practice — CASK/RASK framing used in the calculator tools.
  • DN engagement data — anonymised patterns from diagnostics run with carriers in Africa and the Middle East.

6. Limitations — read this

The Scorecard is a self-assessment screening instrument, not an audit. Scores reflect the knowledge and candour of the person answering. It does not replace an IOSA audit, a regulator inspection or financial due diligence — it tells you where to look first. Results are indicative and carry no certification value.

Your answers never leave your browser — there is no server, database or analytics collecting them. See the Privacy Notice for the full explanation.
